Shenandoah

DCI 190: DCI Studio (1 credit)
Winter Term ~ 2020

Schedule Assignments

This lab course introduces students to the technical aspects of publishing an online literary magazine. Using Shenandoah as a model, the course takes a deep dive into a complex Web publication powered by WordPress. Through closely examining how design and content are enabled by code, students will gain experience in technical writing through documenting the technical architecture of Shenandoah. Students also will master project management through GitHub. While prior technical skills are appreciated, students are expected to bring a strong interest in literature and creative writing. The course culminates in the publication of the Spring issue of Shenandoah.

Attendance

Attendance and full participation in each class session is expected. Since the class meets only once per week, each unexcused absence will lower your final grade by 1/3, e.g., one unexcused absence would make an A- into a B+. The only absences that will be excused are those that are personal illnesses (as indicated from the university health center) or family emergency. Absences for other personal reasons will not be considered excused.

Accomodations

Washington and Lee University makes reasonable academic accommodations for qualified students with disabilities. All undergraduate accommodations must be approved through the Title IX Coordinator and Director of Disability Resources, Elrod Commons 212, (540) 458-4055. Students requesting accommodations for this course should present an official accommodation letter within the first two weeks of the term and schedule a meeting outside of class time to discuss accommodations. It is the student’s responsibility to present this paperwork in a timely fashion and to follow up about accommodation arrangements. Accommodations for test-taking must be arranged with the professor at least a week before the date of the test or exam, including finals.

If you have already been approved for accommodations, please meet with me within the first two weeks of the term so we can develop an implementation plan together. It is important to meet as early in the term as possible; this will ensure that your accommodations are implemented early on.

Grading

Each assignment is worth a specific number of points. It is important to note that you earn points. That means everyone starts with 0 points. At the end of the term, the points you’ve earned will be added and then divided by the total possible points to determine a percentage, which determines your grade according to the following scale:

A = 100 – 93.5%
A - = 93.49 – 90%
B+ = 89.99 – 87%
B = 86.99 – 83%
B - = 82.99 – 80%
C+ = 79.99 – 77%
C = 76.99 – 73%
C - = 72.99 – 70%
D+ = 69.99 – 67.5%
D = 67.49 – 63%
D - = 62.99 – 60%
F = below 60

You must complete all of the assignments in the course. If you do not, you will receive an F.

All work is due at the time and date specified. Any late work will receive a 10% reduction in the grade.

Academic integrity: All work must be your own. You cannot copy ideas or text from another student or from any other source. To avoid questions of plagiarism, you are encouraged to consult resources offered by W&L on understanding and avoiding plagiarism. If you have doubts or questions about your work, please feel free to discuss matters with me. This course will uphold and abide by the W&L Honor Code.
