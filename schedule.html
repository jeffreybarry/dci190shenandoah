1st class ~ January 9

Introductions. Overview of the course and expectations. Establishing timeline for production. Organizing teams and discussing deliverables.

We'll also discuss technical writing. We'll talk about how we write about what we do so that we can remember, understand, explain, and track our work.

For next week:

  • review the Shenandoah site
  • signup for a free GitHub account
  • post 2 discussion questions about Shenandoah

Week 1 ~ January 16

Topic: Project management and communication, essential elements in getting the job done.

Class discussion about your submitted questions about Shenandoah and GitHub.

Last 30 minutes of class: meet with your team to plan your work for the term.

Professor Barry will meet with the project management team to help prepare them for their presentation the following week.

For next week:

  • write a reflection on what tools, particulary software (if any), that help you get your work done and stay organized
  • post 2 discussion questions about project management that you would like the project management team to address

Week 2 ~ January 23

Project Management team presentation and discussion.

Publishing with WordPress. WordPress as a publishing tool: why, pros, cons, and what is multisite WP.

For next week:

  • write a reflection on your experiences with Wordpress (if any); identify at least two websites that you believe are using WP as their content management system
  • post 2 questions about what you would like to learn more about WordPress; at least 1 question should be specific to the use of WordPress in publishing Shenandoah

Week 3 ~ January 30

Topic: the role of documentation

Overview of Shenandoah's technical ecosystem. Exploring the relationship in WP among themes, plugins, sites, content, and code.

For next week:

  • write a reflection distinguishing the roles of themes and plugins in WP. Address how plugins add value to a WP site.
  • identify 2 email newsletters that you like

Week 4 ~ February 6

Topic: the purposes served by email newsletters

The structure of a Shenandoah issue.

For next week:

  • reflection— from your perspective what are the disadvantages of email newsletters and what are alternatives that you might like to see?
  • post 2 questions for the documentation team

Week 5 ~ February 13

Documentation team presentation and discussion

Preparing a new issue of Shenandoah

Discussion of your reflections on newsletters

For next week:
